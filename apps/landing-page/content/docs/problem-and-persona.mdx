# Problem & Persona

## Core problem statement

Delivery workers have variable, fragile weekly income. Their earnings depend on:

1. Being online
2. Receiving orders
3. Being able to move safely in roads and zones

If any of these is interrupted by weather, AQI, or restrictions, earnings can drop immediately.

The problem is not job loss. It is **temporary earning capacity disruption** with immediate cash-flow impact.

## Why existing insurance is insufficient

- No clear physical damage or injury event for standard claims
- Income loss is often short, frequent, and operational
- Manual claims create delay and high friction

Myrva addresses this through predefined event thresholds and automated claim execution.

## Target persona

**Delivery Partner (Swiggy / Zomato)**

- Works long online hours
- Weekly payouts from platform
- Low cash buffer
- High dependence on predictable daily output

## Income break mechanics

Income failure typically happens from one of these patterns:

- **Mobility disruption:** flood, heavy rain, road blocks, curfew
- **Demand / zone disruption:** zone closure or platform-side disruption signal
- **Health-risk environment:** severe AQI and heatwave conditions reducing workable hours

## Product design implications

Because losses are fast and short-cycle, the system must be:

- Trigger-based, not claim-form dependent
- Zone-aware and time-aware
- Weekly-priced and worker-specific
- Fraud-resilient without blocking valid payouts
