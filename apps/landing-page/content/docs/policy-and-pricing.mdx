# Policy & Pricing

## Policy plan

- **Plan:** Delivery Partner Income Shield
- **Enrollment window:** 2 months
- **Premium cadence:** weekly

This structure aligns with the weekly income rhythm of gig delivery workers.

## Weekly premium model

### Step 1 — Composite disruption loss fraction

`L_f = 1 - Π(i=1..n) (1 - P_i × S_i)`

Where each disruption contributes by probability and severity for the worker’s zone and time windows.

### Step 2 — Final weekly premium

`Pr_final = (E_w × L_f × C_t) × (1 + M)`

| Variable | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| P_i | Probability of disruption i in worker’s zone during current week |
| S_i | Severity of disruption i (includes timing effect; peak-hour impact is larger) |
| E_w | Expected weekly earnings baseline from activity history |
| C_t | Tier coverage factor (replacement proportion by tier) |
| M | Risk pool margin to maintain platform solvency |

## Tier definitions

| Tier | Covered Events | Intended Segment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Basic | Severe weather (rain, flood, heat) | Essential protection users |
| Standard | Basic + AQI events | Full-time urban workers |
| Premium | Standard + government-declared disruptions | High-risk zones / high-earning weeks |

## Event-level coverage matrix

| Disruption | Basic | Standard | Premium |
| --- | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Heavy Rain | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Flood | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Extreme Heat | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Hazardous AQI | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Severe AQI | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Government Curfew | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| NDMA Disaster | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Local Strike | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Zone Closure | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |

## Explicit non-coverage

The policy excludes all of the following across tiers:

- Vehicle repair
- Fuel costs
- Device damage
- Platform-side order cancellations (without trigger event)
- Personal illness

## Pricing calibration strategy

`C_t` and `M` are tuned as a constrained optimization problem:

- Maximize affordability and competitiveness
- Maintain acceptable ruin probability for risk pool
- Preserve surplus for correlated events (e.g., city-wide weather shocks)

Historical disruption simulations (Monte Carlo) are used to evaluate robustness before production deployment.
